The world of Valeria uses a twenty-sided die (d20) as the core of all action resolution. When a player attempts anything with a chance of failure, the Dungeon Master calls for an ability check, an attack roll, or a saving throw. The player rolls 1d20, adds the relevant modifier, and compares the total against a Difficulty Class (DC). Easy tasks are DC 10, moderate tasks are DC 13, hard tasks are DC 16, and nearly impossible feats are DC 20.

# Character Classes

## The Wizard

Wizards are masters of arcane magic. They begin with 15 HP, deal high damage, and fight at range.

Starting statistics:

* Hit Points: 15
* Attack bonus: +5 with spells, +1 with weapons
* Damage: 2d8 with Arcane Bolt, 1d4 with staff
* Special resource: 5 mana points per day

Wizard abilities:

* Arcane Bolt (1 mana): ranged attack, 2d8 damage
* Fireball (3 mana): hits all enemies in an area, 3d6 damage each
* Shield (1 mana): +4 to defense until next turn
* Teleport (2 mana): move anywhere within sight

Wizards regain all mana after a full night's rest at an inn or campsite.

## The Warrior

Warriors are masters of raw power. They begin with 30 HP, deal medium damage, and fight in close combat.

Starting statistics:

* Hit Points: 30
* Attack bonus: +5 with melee weapons
* Damage: 2d6 with greatsword, 1d8 with shortsword and shield
* Special resource: 3 stamina points per day

Warrior abilities:

* Power Strike (1 stamina): add +1d6 damage to a melee attack
* Shield Wall (1 stamina): halve all damage taken until next turn
* Battle Cry (2 stamina): all allies gain +2 to attack for 3 rounds
* Second Wind (1 stamina): recover 1d8 HP, once per combat

## The Assassin

Assassins are masters of shadow. They begin with 12 HP, deal fatal damage from stealth, and fight in close combat.

Starting statistics:

* Hit Points: 12
* Attack bonus: +6 with daggers
* Damage: 1d6 normally, 4d6 on a sneak attack
* Special resource: 4 shadow points per day

Assassin abilities:

* Sneak Attack (free): 4d6 damage if the target has not seen the assassin this combat
* Vanish (1 shadow): become hidden, enemies lose track of you
* Poison Blade (2 shadow): target takes 1d4 damage per round for 3 rounds
* Shadow Step (1 shadow): move behind any enemy within sight

# Combat Rules

## Initiative

When combat begins, every participant rolls 1d20. Turns proceed from the highest roll to the lowest. On a tie, players act before monsters.

## The Combat Round

On their turn, a character may move and take one action. Actions include:

* Attack: roll 1d20 + attack bonus against the target's defense
* Use an ability: spend the resource and resolve the effect
* Defend: gain +4 defense until your next turn
* Use an item: drink a potion, throw a flask, pull a lever
* Flee: attempt to escape, requires a DC 12 check

## Attack Resolution

An attack hits if 1d20 + attack bonus meets or exceeds the target's defense value. On a hit, roll the weapon's damage dice and subtract the total from the target's HP.

A natural 20 on the die is a critical hit: roll double the damage dice. A natural 1 is a fumble: the attack misses and the DM may impose a small mishap.

## Death and Dying

A character reduced to 0 HP falls unconscious. Each round they must roll 1d20: on 10 or higher they stabilize, on 9 or lower they lose the roll. Three failed rolls mean death. Any healing restores an unconscious character to consciousness.

# Monsters of Valeria

## Goblin

* HP: 7, Defense: 12
* Attack: +3, Damage: 1d6 (rusty blade)
* Goblins are cowardly: if half the group falls, the rest flee.

## Orc Raider

* HP: 15, Defense: 13
* Attack: +5, Damage: 1d12 (greataxe)
* Orcs fight to the death and always attack the strongest-looking enemy.

## Shadow Wraith

* HP: 20, Defense: 15
* Attack: +6, Damage: 2d6 (life drain, heals the wraith for half)
* Immune to normal weapons; only magic or silvered blades harm it.

## Young Dragon

* HP: 60, Defense: 17
* Attack: +8, Damage: 2d10 (bite) or Fire Breath: 4d6 to all in a line, once every 3 rounds
* Dragons hoard treasure. Defeating one yields triple the normal loot roll.

# Leveling and Experience

Characters earn experience points (XP) for defeating monsters and completing quests:

* Goblin: 25 XP
* Orc Raider: 75 XP
* Shadow Wraith: 150 XP
* Young Dragon: 500 XP
* Minor quest: 100 XP
* Major quest: 300 XP

Level thresholds:

* Level 2: 300 XP
* Level 3: 900 XP
* Level 4: 2000 XP
* Level 5: 4000 XP

At each level, a character gains: +5 maximum HP (warrior), +3 maximum HP (wizard, assassin), +1 to attack bonus, and +1 point of their special resource.

# Treasure and Loot

After a victorious combat, the party rolls 1d20 on the loot table:

* 1-5: 2d6 gold coins
* 6-10: 4d6 gold coins and a common item
* 11-15: a healing potion and 3d6 gold coins
* 16-19: an uncommon magic item
* 20: a rare magic item chosen by the DM

Common items include torches, rope, rations, and simple weapons. Healing potions restore 2d4+2 HP when drunk as an action.

Notable magic items of Valeria:

* Blade of the North Wind: a shortsword that deals +1d4 cold damage
* Cloak of Whispers: grants advantage on stealth checks
* Ring of the Ember: allows casting Fireball once per day without mana
* Amulet of Thornhold: +2 defense while worn

***

# Magic and Rest

Spellcasters recover all mana, stamina, and shadow points after a long rest of eight hours. A short rest of one hour allows each character to recover 1d8 HP once per day.

Resting in the wilderness carries risk: the DM rolls 1d20, and on 5 or lower the camp is ambushed by wandering monsters.

Inns in Thornhold charge 5 gold coins per night and guarantee a safe long rest, plus a free breakfast rumored to be the best in the kingdom.

***

# Skill Checks

Outside of combat, common checks include:

* Persuasion: convince an NPC (DC set by their attitude)
* Stealth: sneak past guards or monsters (opposed by their perception)
* Lockpicking: open a locked door or chest (DC 12 for simple locks, DC 16 for masterwork)
* Arcana: identify magic items and read ancient runes (wizards get +3)
* Survival: track creatures, find food, avoid getting lost in the wilds

The DM may grant advantage (roll two d20, keep the higher) when circumstances favor the character, or disadvantage (roll two, keep the lower) when they do not.
